  1. Bibimbap


    2. Bibimbap is a Korean rice dish that is made by mixing rice, meat, vegetables, and bibimbap sauce.

    Bibimbap is a Korean rice dish that comes from bibim, meaning “to mix” and bap,
    meaning “rice.” It is usually topped with
    sauteed or seasoned vegetables, kimchi, raw or fried egg,
    and optionally, beef or other meats. To tie it all together, it is served
    with bibimbap sauce, which is mainly based on gochugang or red
    chili paste, and seasoned with soy sauce, sugar, and spices.

  2. Japchae


    3. This Korean glass noodle dish is sweet, savory, and filled with vegetables!

    Japchae comes from jap, meaning “mixed”, and chae, meaning “vegetables”.
    Interestingly, it comes from the same Chinese characters that would later
    be known as chop suey     in Chinese cuisine, but the two dishes couldn’t be
    more different! Whereas chop suey is a stir-fried vegetable dish, japchae is a
    Korean glass noodle dish that is seasoned with a light sweet soy sauce with
    stir-fried vegetables.

  3. Bulgogi


    4. Bulgogi is usually made with beef, but you can also make pork and chicken bulgogi!

    Bulgogi is a grilled or roasted Korean meat dish, which can also be stir-fried at home.
    It comes from bul, meaning “fire”, and gogi, meaning “meat”.
    Traditionally, it is prepared with prime beef cuts like thinly-sliced sirloin, but
    it can also be made with pork and chicken. It’s a quick Korean food recipe to
    make as you can prepare ahead of time by mixing your choice of meat with
    Korean bulgogi marinade, and then simply stir-frying the meat with garlic
    and other spices.

  4. Tteokbokki


    5. Tteokbokki are chewy rice cakes that can be prepared with a variety of sauces.

    One of the most popular Korean food is tteokbokki (pronounced: tuk-boh-kee),
    which is made with tteok or rice cakes, that are simmered or stir-fried
    in a sauce. Bokki means “something fried”, and depending on the sauce, you
    can change the flavor profile of these soft and chewy rice cakes drastically.
    The most famous version these days is made with sweet and spicy
    gochujang sauce, with eomuk or fish cake slices, egg, scallions, and garlic
    added. However, it can be made with a variety of other sauces, or even
    mixed with ramyun or ramen!

  5. Kimbap

    6. Kimbap are Korean rice rolls that are usually eaten as a snack or a meal on-
    the-go.     They are made by using kim or dried seaweed to wrap the bap or
    cooked rice and a variety of fillings. The fillings usually include danmuji or
    yellow pickled radish, egg strips, kimchi, seasoned or stir-fried vegetables,
    and meat such as ham, kani or imitation crab, or bulgogi. It is a versatile rice
    roll that is not only a highly customizable Korean recipe but it is also quite
    portable and easy to make.
    It may look a lot like the Japanese sushi rolls, but kimbap cannot be more
    different. Whereas sushi rice is seasoned with vinegar, kimbap is prepared
    by mixing sesame oil with rice. It can also be served as is or with other
    Korean banchan like kimchi.

  6. Samgyupsal Recipes

    7. Samgyupsal, sometimes spelled sampgyeopsal and sometimes called
    samgyeopsal-gui, has become a big hit with Filipino Korean food lovers.
    Samgyupsal means “three-layered flesh”, which refers to the layers you see
    when you cut into a pork belly. Gui means “to grill”; samgyupsal literally just
    refers to pork belly (the pork cut), while samgyupsal-gui is the full term
    when you want to refer to the grilled pork belly dish.

  7. Korean noodles (Ramyun, Jajangmyeon, Jambbong)


    8. Jajangmyeon or black bean noodles is just one of the most popular noodle-based Korean comfort food.

    Korean noodle dishes are so diverse, but some of the most popular ones are ramyun, jajangmyeon, and jambbong or jjamppong.

    Ramyun is most commonly known as a Korean instant noodle dish that is served with a signature red spicy broth. Unlike the Japanese ramen, is usually prepared fresh, ramyun is a classic Korean comfort food made with instant noodles that can be topped with egg and elevated with meat or other vegetables.

    Jajangmyeon is a Korean black bean noodle dish that is made with chunjang or black bean paste and meats like diced pork or seafood and vegetables. It is considered as a Chinese-style Korean dish, but since its introduction to Korean culture in the 19th century, it has cemented its place as one of the most beloved Korean noodle dishes. It is popular not only for its unique taste but also because jajjangmyeon ingredients are relatively cheap and easy to prepare.

    Lastly, jambbong or jjamppong is a Korean seafood noodle soup that is known not just for its spiciness but also for the fresh seafood flavors of its broth. It is also considered to be a Chinese-Korean dish, and is usually topped with Korean zucchini, squid, mussels, and pork, with a seafood or pork-based broth that is colored red and made spicy with the addition of gochugaru.
